Chai (चाय) is the Hindi word for tea, and it's a deeply cherished beverage in many parts of the world, especially in India and South Asia. Here's a bit about chai:
Origin: Chai originated in India and has become a cultural staple, often prepared by boiling black tea leaves with water, milk, sugar, and spices.

Masala Chai: The most iconic version of chai is masala chai, which includes a blend of spices like cardamom, cinnamon, ginger, cloves, and black pepper. It’s warming and aromatic.

Social Ritual: In India, chai is more than just a drink; it’s a way of life. People often gather around chai stalls, known as chai wallahs, to chat and share stories.
Health Benefits: Chai spices like ginger and cardamom are known for their health benefits, including aiding digestion, boosting immunity, and reducing inflammation. Global Popularity: While chai is traditional to South Asia, it has gained immense popularity worldwide. However, in many Western countries, it’s often referred to as "chai tea," which technically means "tea tea." Preparation Variations: Chai preparation can vary by region and household. Some prefer it spicier, others sweeter, and some even add a pinch of salt. Pairings: Chai is often enjoyed with snacks like biscuits, samosas, pakoras, or savory street food.
